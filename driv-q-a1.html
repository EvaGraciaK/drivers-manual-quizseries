Age Restrictions --- Drivers 16-21

1. Take Driver`s Ed, do 50 hr of practice driving, and pass the driver`s license exam

2. A legal guardian or responsible adult over 21 years old must sign an Affidavit/Consent for Minor to Drive

3. Complete a 6 hr Adult Driver Education Course through a State Secretary certified adult driver education provider

4. A hired vehicle transporting property and a vehicle requiring a commercial driver`s license

5. hired vehicle with over 10 passengers, commuter van, religious organization bus, school bus, vehicle transporting senior citizens, and child care vehicle

Bonus facts: Look up the 2024 Illinois Rules of the Road, and scroll down to Chapter 1, section "Exemptions" (p. 5). Read what it says.

Changing the Name/Address on an Illinois Driver`s License/ID Card

1. Apply for a corrected driver`s license/ID card

2. 30 days

3. Documentation to link the new name to the name on your current driver`s license/ID card and on the file with the State Secretary`s office

4. Your full given name and surname at birth, marriage, assigned by the Social Security Administration, or otherwise established via legal action

5. 10 days

6. Visit a driver`s facility, visit ilsos.gov online services, or write him a letter

Helpful note: The State Secretary`s address: Secretary of State, Driver Services Department, Address Change, 2701 S. Dirksen Pkwy., Springfield, IL 62723.

7. Visit a facility, present acceptable documents to prove residency including a valid mailing address, and pay the appropriate fee

Additional facts: See page 105 of the 2024 driver`s manual for acceptable documents.

Document/ID Requirements for Driver`s License/ID Card

1. Your legal name, birthdate, SSN, residency, and signature

3. Provide valid mailing address, have your picture taken, surrender all other driver`s licenses, ID cards, and instruction permits, pass the appropriate exams, and pay the appropriate fee

4. Identity proof, proof of birthdate, proof of SSN, proof of signature, and 2 documents as proof of Illinois residency

5. Proof of birthdate, proof of SSN, one document as proof of residency, and proof of signature

6. Proof of written signature, and proof of residency in the case of an address change

7. Address, name, and birthdate

8. Name, birthdate, SSN, or gender

9. A police report that states that their driver`s license/ID card was stolen

Driver`s License/ID Card Renewal

1. 90 days

2. 1 yr

3. 6 months

4. 4 yrs

5. On your birthday

6. The license of a driver under age 21, the license of drivers ages 81-86, drivers ages 87+, and temporary visitor driver`s licenses

7. 3 months after their 21st birthday

8. 2 yrs

9. 1 yr

10. Either after 3 yrs or when the owner`s immigration documents expire

11. (1) Visit a Driver Services facility and present your current driver`s license/ID card, or (2) visit ilsos.gov online services if you received a pin renewal notice, (3) take any appropriate exams, and (4) pay the appropriate fee and have your new picture taken

12. Proof of signature

13. Proof of birthdate, SSN, proof of residency (1 document for a standard applicant or 2 for a REAL ID applicant), and proof of written signature

14. After paying the fees and completing required testing, and for 90 days

15. At the address you provide, usually within 15 business days

16. 5 days a week, excluding holidays and special days off

17. Check the status at ilsos.gov or call 217-782-7044

18. Renew their licenses from home and only visit a Driver Services facility once every 8 yrs

Penalties for Driver`s License/ID Card Fraud

1. Signing a driver`s license/ID card application with false info, presenting false info to get a driver`s license/ID card, using a fake or illegally altered driver`s license/permit, presenting someone else`s driver`s license/ID card as your own, and allowing another person to knowingly use your identification documents to apply for a driver`s license/ID card using their own name

2. Arrest, possible imprisonment for up to 5 yrs, and a 12-month suspension/revocation of their driving privileges

Medical Report

1. Some physical/mental conditions, certain medications, drugs, and alcohol

2. Medical Reports are to ensure your medical ability to drive safely, and you must present it within 90 days after a physician signs it

3. Any conditions you develop that could make you pass out or lose any ability to drive safely, and tell him within 10 days of finding out

4. Your driver`s license and driving privileges may be cancelled

Non-Driving Programs Related to the Driver`s Licensed

1. Illinois Tissue/Donor Registry, Veteran Designation, Voter Registration, Selective Service, and Emergency Contact Database

2. Driver`s license/ID card applicants ages 16+

3. A special designation on your driver`s license/ID card that tells everyone that you`re a veteran

4. Provide proof of an honorable discharge from the military or retirement from the military, or show service as a reservist or National Guard member

5. Whether you`re currently registered to vote and at what address

6. Whether you want to register or update your registration address

7. The applicant will be advised that their driver`s license/ID card application will also be used for their voter registration, and the info will be electronically sent to the Illinois Board of Elections, which will then forward it to the appropriate local election authority, unless the applicant opts out

8. All males ages 18-25 have to register with the Selective Service System

9. Their signatures certify that they`ve registered, or allows the State Secretary`s office to submit his info to the Selective Service System to be registered

10. It lets people provide emergency contact info for law enforcement or emergency responders if there`s a crash or emergency, and it also lets them provide info concerning disabilities or special medical needs
