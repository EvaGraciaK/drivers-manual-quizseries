Proper Action When Stopped by Law Enforcement

  1. . Pull over, stay in the vehicle with both hands visible on the steering wheel, be ready for the police officer to approach from either side of your vehicle, and obey the officer`s request to see your driver`s license and proof of insurance
    1. . Slow down, use turn signal, and pull over safely
      1. . The righthand shoulder of the roadway
      2. . If there`s no shoulder or it`s too narrow, pull over at the next safest location
    2. . When the police officer instructs otherwise, or when the traffic stop is over
    3. . Let the police officer know and follow his/her directions before getting the driver`s license and proof of insurance
  2. . Slam on the brakes, stop in the traffic lane or other places that may be risky, or open or exit your vehicle
    1. . On bridges, curves, next to guardrails, concrete walls, medians, too close to the solid white line, or at any other place where it would be hard for other drivers to pass you
    2. . It may be perceived as aggressive behavior and a threat to the police officer
  3. . Pull over at a well-lit, populated location; drive carefully to the nearest police station and try to attract a uniformed police officer`s attention; or call 911
  4. . Turn on your hazard lights to help the police officer know your intent
  7. . Your driver`s license, vehicle registration, and insurance card
    1. . Don`t debate the reason for the traffic stop or argue with the police, don`t refuse to sign a ticket if given one, don`t be uncooperative in any tests the police tell you to take, don`t argue about any ticket when it`s given to you, and don`t resist arrest if arrested
